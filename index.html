Virat Kohli gets GOAT icon on Twitter, what does it mean?

The Great Kohli a.k.a. King Kohli In 2019, Virat Kohli was awarded the coveted Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for the second year in a row, also named as the ICC ODI and Test Cricketer of 2018. He became the first cricketer to do so in the same calendar year. The 33-year-old, has returned to form with this dominating performance in the initial game of the T20 World Cup. Without a doubt, King Kohli also enjoyed a great phase of his career as both captain and batsman over the years. His dominance with the willow has always been a topic of discussion among the cricket fraternity while his game reached new levels. At that time, Twitter introduced the latest GOAT icon on its official handle and the ICC took the opportunity to add more fuel to the debate by asking the cricket fraternity and fans whether Kohli has called himself the ‘Greatest of All Time’ (GOAT) has been established. Former England captain Michael Vaughan, who is a vocal figure on Twitter, referred to Kohli as the ‘GOAT’ (Greatest of All Time). When a user asked in the comments as to whether Vaughan was indicating that Kohli was better than the likes of Australian legend Sir Don Bradman, West Indies great Brian Lara and Tendulkar, Vaughan replied, “In One day cricket … YES …”
